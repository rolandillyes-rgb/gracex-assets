The Platform

What GraceX is

GraceX is a psychosocial risk management platform built entirely inside Microsoft 365. It gives business leaders a structured, documented approach to their WHS obligations under Australian psychosocial hazard legislation and the 2024 Code of Practice, without adding new software, new logins, or new systems.

It operates through Teams, SharePoint, and Forms that businesses already pay for. A human expert reviews anonymous survey results each month and provides a written report with specific leadership actions. Leaders see trends over time in Power BI. All data stays inside the customer's own Microsoft 365 tenant at all times.

Three pillars

Weekly structured content posted to a dedicated Teams channel. Evidence-based, practical material covering psychosocial risk topics is delivered to the whole team every week without any manual effort from the leader. Content is written by the GraceX expert team and calibrated to the Australian WHS context.

Fortnightly anonymous survey cycle delivered via Microsoft Forms. Staff complete short surveys in the tools they already use. De-identified results are reviewed monthly by a human expert who produces a written report with specific, actionable recommendations for leadership.

Leaders see aggregated trends over time in a Power BI dashboard. No personal data leaves the customer's tenant. No algorithmic scoring. A qualified professional reads the data and writes the report.

A SharePoint-based engagement and recognition layer. Staff earn credits for participating in surveys and Check-In content. Credits are redeemable against employer-funded rewards. Leaders see participation rates alongside survey sentiment, giving visibility of engagement as well as content.

Readiness Assessment

Before GraceX is deployed, every customer completes a Readiness Assessment. This is a structured review across ten domains covering governance, psychosocial risk management, policies, culture, workload, communication, onboarding, leave and flexibility, recognition, and supply chain. A trained assessor works through the domains with the customer across a series of sessions, drawing on documents, data, and structured conversations with key people. The output is a written report covering current state across all ten domains, prioritised recommendations, and an implementation roadmap that sequences the work before and during GraceX rollout. The assessment is a standalone product priced according to the complexity of the organisation and the scope of work required. It is not a bolt-on or an optional step. This step is crucial for the solid foundation.

Important distinctions
Not an AI tool or chatbot

GraceX does not use artificial intelligence, machine learning, or automated decision-making. All analysis and recommendations come from a human expert.

Not an EAP or counselling service

GraceX is a risk management and compliance platform. It does not provide individual counselling, crisis support, or clinical services of any kind.

Not an algorithmic monitoring system

GraceX does not score, rank, or flag individual employees. Survey responses are de-identified. The platform monitors organisational patterns, not individuals.

Not a consulting engagement

GraceX is a subscription platform, not a project or consulting arrangement. There are no variable day rates, no scope creep, and no dependency on consultant availability.

Not new software to install

GraceX requires no new applications, no app store downloads, and no IT department involvement. It is deployed entirely within the customer's existing Microsoft 365 environment.

Not a replacement for EAP

GraceX and EAP serve different purposes. EAP is a reactive support service. GraceX is a proactive risk management and compliance system. They are complementary, not substitutes.
