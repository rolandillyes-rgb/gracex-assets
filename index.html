The Platform

What GraceX is

GraceX is a psychosocial risk management platform built entirely inside Microsoft 365. It gives business leaders a structured, documented approach to their WHS obligations under Australian psychosocial hazard legislation and the 2024 Code of Practice, without adding new software, new logins, or new systems.

It operates through Teams, SharePoint, and Forms that businesses already pay for. A human expert reviews anonymous survey results each month and provides a written report with specific leadership actions. Leaders see trends over time in Power BI. All data stays inside the customer's own Microsoft 365 tenant at all times.

Three pillars

Weekly structured content posted to a dedicated Teams channel. Evidence-based, practical material covering psychosocial risk topics is delivered to the whole team every week without any manual effort from the leader. Content is written by the GraceX expert team and calibrated to the Australian WHS context.

Fortnightly anonymous survey cycle delivered via Microsoft Forms. Staff complete short surveys in the tools they already use. De-identified results are reviewed monthly by a human expert who produces a written report with specific, actionable recommendations for leadership.

Leaders see aggregated trends over time in a Power BI dashboard. No personal data leaves the customer's tenant. No algorithmic scoring. A qualified professional reads the data and writes the report.

A SharePoint-based engagement and recognition layer. Staff earn credits for participating in surveys and Check-In content. Credits are redeemable against employer-funded rewards. Leaders see participation rates alongside survey sentiment, giving visibility of engagement as well as content.

Readiness Assessment

Before GraceX is deployed, every customer completes a Readiness Assessment. This is a structured review across ten domains covering governance, psychosocial risk management, policies, culture, workload, communication, onboarding, leave and flexibility, recognition, and supply chain. A trained assessor works through the domains with the customer across a series of sessions, drawing on documents, data, and structured conversations with key people. The output is a written report covering current state across all ten domains, prioritised recommendations, and an implementation roadmap that sequences the work before and during GraceX rollout. The assessment is a standalone product priced according to the complexity of the organisation and the scope of work required. It is not a bolt-on or an optional step. No customer proceeds to GraceX deployment without one.

Partner FAQ

Questions partners ask

  • High staff turnover. Employee turnover above 5% in the last 12 months, or a business that appears to be in a permanent recruitment cycle. A fast-growth or scale-up phase produces the same conditions and is equally strong as an entry point.
  • Known absenteeism problem. If the business recognises that unplanned leave is an issue, particularly where it is running above seven days per employee per year on average, that points to an underlying problem GraceX is designed to surface.
  • Approaching an M&A. Pre-transaction, GraceX can support the psychosocial risk component of due diligence. This is a credible and specific entry point with boards and acquirers.
  • Post-M&A integration. After a transaction closes, staff navigate uncertainty, leadership change, and cultural adjustment. The psychosocial risk profile of the combined business is elevated. Leaders often do not have visibility of it.
  • Board requesting psychosocial risk reporting. If the board has asked for this, the CEO needs a documented, defensible response. GraceX provides exactly that.
  • Workers compensation renewal within three months. Insurers are increasingly pricing psychosocial risk. A business approaching renewal has a direct financial reason to act.

The common thread is a CEO or MD who either knows they have a problem or is about to be asked to demonstrate they have one under control.

  • No new software. GraceX runs entirely inside Microsoft 365 tools the business already uses. Nothing to install, no new software to licence, no IT project to manage.
  • Data never leaves the customer's tenant. All data stays inside the customer's own Microsoft 365 environment at all times. GraceX holds nothing. Only de-identified survey summaries reach the expert for review.
  • Human expert, not an algorithm. Survey results are reviewed by a human organisational psychologist. The monthly report and the leadership actions that come with it are the product of professional judgement, not automated scoring or pattern detection.
  • Structured to the 2024 WHS Code of Practice. GraceX is designed specifically around the Australian Code of Practice for Managing Psychosocial Hazards at Work. Customers build a defensible, documented compliance posture, not just a survey habit.
  • Continuous, not one-off. GraceX operates fortnightly. The result is an ongoing evidence trail of consultation, action, and review that a business can demonstrate to a regulator.
  • Built for the business owner. Designed for a CEO or MD carrying personal WHS liability, not an HR specialist. Plain language, specific actions, Power BI visibility. No specialist knowledge required to operate it.
  • Not an AI tool or chatbot. GraceX does not use artificial intelligence, machine learning, or automated decision-making of any kind. All analysis and recommendations come from a human expert.
  • Not an algorithmic monitoring system. GraceX does not score, rank, or flag individual employees. Survey responses are de-identified. The platform captures anonymous responses at the organisational level, not individual behaviour.
  • Not an EAP or counselling service. GraceX does not provide individual counselling, crisis support, or clinical services of any kind. If a business already has an EAP, GraceX works alongside it.
  • Not a consulting engagement. GraceX is a subscription platform, not a project or advisory arrangement. There are no variable day rates, no scope creep, and no dependency on consultant availability.
  • Not a one-off assessment. GraceX operates continuously. The Readiness Assessment is a prerequisite product, not the platform itself. The subscription runs fortnightly, month after month.
  • Not new software to install. GraceX requires no new applications, no app store downloads, and no IT department involvement. It is deployed entirely within the customer's existing Microsoft 365 environment.
